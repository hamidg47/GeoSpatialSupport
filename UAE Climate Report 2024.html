UAE Climate Change Report 2024

The UAE Climate Change Report 2024 outlines key environmental shifts affecting the region. It includes updated research on rising temperatures, water scarcity, carbon emissions, and government strategies for climate resilience and sustainability. It is a vital document for policymakers, researchers, and citizens alike.

Major Findings

▽

Precipitation

• UAE experiences low and erratic rainfall (78–120 mm annually), with extreme precipitation events recorded in February and April 2024.

• Significant increases in precipitation observed in Al Ain (500%), Dubai (450%), and Fujairah (350%) compared to the 20-year average.

• 3Climate change has led to unpredictable rainfall patterns, posing challenges for water resource management and flood risk mitigation.

Flooding

• Flooding events in February and April 2024 affected large areas, with Al Ain (588 km²) experiencing the largest impact.

• Causes include extreme weather, urbanization, inadequate drainage, and geographical factors like dry riverbeds.

• Remote sensing techniques like SAR and CCD-PCA were effective for flood mapping and disaster response.

• Dams play a crucial role in flood control and water storage, holding over 114 million cubic meters of water across 200+ structures.

Groundwater

• The UAE’s aquifers (limestone, gravel plain, ophiolite, and sand-dune) are under severe pressure from over-extraction and climate change.

• Groundwater storage declined significantly from 2002 to 2024, with a total drop of -9 cm.

• Machine learning models (Random Forest and LSTM) accurately predicted groundwater trends using remote sensing data.

• Desalination and treated wastewater are essential for non-potable uses but require careful management.

Sea Level Rise

• UAE’s 34,000 km coastline, with 18,000 km populated, faces significant risks from rising sea levels.

• A 0.5m sea-level rise could impact 5% of infrastructure, and a 3m rise could flood 40% of urban areas.

• More than 85% of the UAE’s population lives along the coastline, increasing vulnerability to flooding and displacement.

• Rising sea levels could also lead to groundwater salinization, affecting agriculture and drinking water sources.

Population Growth and Urbanization

• The UAE experienced the highest global population growth rate from 1975 to 2005, increasing the strain on infrastructure and water resources.

• Urban sprawl into natural floodplains has further heightened the risk of flood disasters..

GIS and Remote Sensing (RS) Applications

• GIS, remote sensing, and ground surveys can effectively identify high-risk areas and prioritize protective measures.

• Authorities can leverage these technologies for disaster planning and climate change adaptation.

Technological advancements to address climate change in the UAE

▽

Climate Model for UAE

• Global & Regional Climate Models (GCMs & RCMs): These are essential for studying large-scale trends and regional variability.

• Recommendation: A dedicated RCM for UAE would improve analysis of local climate variability and extremes.

Precipitation Data

• Challenges: Rainfall is scarce and variable in arid regions like the UAE, complicating prediction efforts.

Solutions:

  • Integrating satellite precipitation data with ground measurements to enhance accuracy.
  • Easier and free access to rain gauge data for research and modeling.
  • Expanding rain gauge stations to improve spatial data coverage.

Flash Flood Management

• Flood Forecasting & Mitigation: Effective strategies include early warning systems, risk mapping, and emergency preparedness.

Technological Solutions:

  • High-resolution flood simulations, integrating satellite data and ground observations, have shown accuracy in runoff predictions.
  • Flood control measures like detention basins and dry dams reduce flood damage significantly.
  • Urban drainage alternatives (e.g., porous paving, vegetated swales) promote infiltration and reduce runoff.
  • High-resolution Digital Elevation Models (DEMs) are essential for accurate hydrological modeling.

Groundwater Monitoring

• Groundwater Recharge & Abstraction:Natural recharge is minimal compared to total abstraction, posing sustainability concerns.

Recommendations:

  • Increasing monitoring well stations for better spatial coverage and validation.
  • Upgrading outdated equipment to improve monitoring reliability, focusing on battery system enhancements.
